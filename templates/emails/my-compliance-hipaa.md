# My Compliance — HIPAA — Outreach template

**Frame:** Healthcare org hiring their first Privacy/Compliance Officer, often pre-OCR-audit or post-near-miss. Pitch: fractional Privacy Officer + full Security Rule / Privacy Rule / Breach Notification alignment — done in weeks not years.

## Subject variants

1. Re: your {{role}} search at {{company}}
2. About the {{role}} JD — quick note
3. HIPAA posture question for {{company}}

## Touch 1 — Day 0

> Hi {{contact_first_name}},
>
> Saw the {{role}} posting. Line "{{jd_quote}}" tells me {{company}} is standing up formal HIPAA governance — usually either post-incident, pre-OCR audit, or because a BAA partner asked hard questions.
>
> Whichever of those it is, a single hire on day one will inherit a pile of risk-assessment, PHI mapping, and BAA gaps that are faster to clear with fractional expertise than alone.
>
> Worth 15 min to map the first 90 days with them already in mind?
>
> {{signature}}

## Touch 2 — Day 4

> {{contact_first_name}} — one thing that catches most first Privacy Officers off guard: the Security Rule risk assessment isn't a questionnaire, it's an analysis backed by evidence. OCR looks for the analysis, not the score. Getting that right in month one matters more than any other single thing.
>
> Worth 15 min?
>
> {{signature}}

## Touch 3 — Day 10

> Did the {{role}} role close? If OCR posture is still open, even a 20-min conversation before the first audit letter saves months.
>
> {{signature}}

## Service-specific DO

- If the JD specifically says "OCR audit prep" → lead with that phrase verbatim
- Reference CMIA if the company is California-based (shows you know the state stack)
- Position as complement to the hire, not replacement — Privacy Officers value peers, not vendors

## Service-specific DON'T

- Do NOT open with scare tactics ("$1.5M penalties per violation"). It's lazy and insulting to the buyer
- Don't name-drop OCR enforcement actions unless they were at a direct peer
- Don't offer a free risk assessment — compliance buyers read "free" as "unqualified"
