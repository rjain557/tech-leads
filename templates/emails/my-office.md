# My Office — Outreach template

**Frame:** Company hiring first M365 admin, or mid-migration from on-prem Exchange or Google Workspace. Also Copilot-curious with no governance in place. Pitch: M365 migration, SharePoint modernization, Teams, security hardening, Copilot pilots — 25+ years of Exchange/SharePoint/Teams delivery.

## Subject variants

1. Re: your {{role}} posting
2. About the {{role}} JD — quick note
3. M365 migration question — {{role}} role

## Touch 1 — Day 0

> Hi {{contact_first_name}},
>
> Saw the {{role}} posting. The "{{jd_quote}}" line reads like {{company}} is either starting an M365 migration or finally modernizing a SharePoint classic sprawl.
>
> We've run these migrations for 25+ years — Exchange on-prem → Online, Google Workspace → M365, SharePoint classic → modern, all with MFA + Conditional Access + Purview posture locked in before day one. One admin hire lands into a modern estate instead of inheriting a pile of governance debt.
>
> Worth 15 min to walk the 90-day plan?
>
> {{signature}}

## Touch 2 — Day 4

> {{contact_first_name}} — the overlooked part of M365 migrations: Conditional Access policies need to land day-one, not post-migration. Phishing campaigns targeting freshly-migrated tenants hit hardest in that first week while the guardrails are still being built. Most admins learn that by getting hit.
>
> Short call?
>
> {{signature}}

## Touch 3 — Day 10

> Did the {{role}} close? If Copilot is on the roadmap, happy to share the governance checklist we use before rollout — no call needed.
>
> {{signature}}

## Service-specific DO

- Lead with migration source → target when there's a signal (Google Workspace, on-prem Exchange)
- Reference Conditional Access, Intune, Purview — specific enough to signal M365 literacy
- Offer Copilot governance as the touch 3 hook if the JD mentions it

## Service-specific DON'T

- Don't pitch generic "M365 help" — the signal told you what the migration is, use it
- Don't mention licensing cost savings in touch 1 — comes across as a license reseller pitch
