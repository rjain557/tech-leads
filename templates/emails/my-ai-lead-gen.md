# My AI Lead Gen — Outreach template

**Frame:** Company hiring SDRs / BDRs / Demand Gen. They're either paying $30K+/yr for ZoomInfo or Apollo and still cold-list fatigued, or they're scaling an outbound team whose hours are eaten by manual list-building. Pitch: fresh, vertical-tuned, Claude-scored leads delivered weekly so SDRs spend time on conversations not spreadsheets.

## Subject variants

1. Re: your {{role}} search at {{company}}
2. About the {{role}} posting — quick thought
3. Saw you're hiring SDRs — one idea

## Touch 1 — Day 0

> Hi {{contact_first_name}},
>
> Saw the {{role}} posting. The line about "{{jd_quote}}" tells me list-building is probably eating 30-40% of the role before a conversation even happens.
>
> We run an AI lead-gen engine that pulls vertical-tuned prospect lists from 10+ public sources (SAM.gov, SBA, UCC, DOT awards, permits) — scored by Claude, delivered weekly, ready to outreach. Teams running it free their SDRs for the part of the job SDRs are good at.
>
> Worth 15 min to see a sample batch for your ICP?
>
> {{signature}}

## Touch 2 — Day 4

> {{contact_first_name}} — if helpful, I can pull a small sample batch of prospects for {{company}}'s ICP from our pipeline (vertical-tuned, Claude-scored, public-source origin). No commitment — just reply and I'll send the raw output so you can line it up against whatever you're running today.
>
> {{signature}}

## Touch 3 — Day 10

> Still scaling the SDR team, {{contact_first_name}}? If the sample didn't land I'll stop chasing — just let me know either way.
>
> {{signature}}

## Service-specific DO

- Offer a concrete sample batch as the CTA — converts better than "book a call" for this persona
- Speak their vocabulary: ICP, cadence, reply rate, seat cost
- If JD mentions Apollo or ZoomInfo specifically, reference it — they're feeling the seat-cost pain already

## Service-specific DON'T

- Don't pitch the service and the lead sample in the same touch — touch 1 is curiosity, touch 2 is proof
- Don't claim specific reply-rate numbers unless you have them for their vertical
