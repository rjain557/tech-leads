# My Cloud — Outreach template

**Frame:** Company hiring Cloud/DevOps/SRE — often a first cloud hire in a regulated vertical, or a VMware-on-prem shop mid-migration to AWS/Azure. Pitch: own SOC 2 datacenter + AWS/Azure expertise, FinOps savings 30-40%, HIPAA-eligible architectures.

## Subject variants

1. Re: your {{role}} search
2. About the {{role}} posting at {{company}}
3. Cloud build question — {{role}} JD

## Touch 1 — Day 0

> Hi {{contact_first_name}},
>
> Saw the {{role}} posting. The combination of "{{jd_quote}}" plus the on-prem references in the JD reads like {{company}} is either mid-migration or about to start one.
>
> Before that hire lands — we've taken a few peer companies through the same VMware-to-cloud transition with 30-40% FinOps savings and HIPAA-eligible posture on day one. Saves a new hire six months of paving cow paths.
>
> Worth 15 min before the role starts?
>
> {{signature}}

## Touch 2 — Day 4

> {{contact_first_name}} — the trap in most VMware-to-cloud migrations: reserved-instance commitments get made before workload profiles are mapped, and teams end up paying for capacity they don't use. Front-loading the profiling step is what makes the 30-40% FinOps number real instead of theoretical.
>
> Short call?
>
> {{signature}}

## Touch 3 — Day 10

> Did the {{role}} role close? If the migration is already underway and you want a second pair of eyes, happy to take a look — no pitch.
>
> {{signature}}

## Service-specific DO

- Lean on the FinOps number — a specific % savings lands harder than "cost optimization"
- If JD mentions VMware + AWS/Azure both, call out the migration tell explicitly
- Reference the SOC 2 datacenter as the differentiator vs. hyperscaler-only vendors

## Service-specific DON'T

- Don't list every cloud tool (Terraform, Kubernetes, Ansible) — reads like a résumé
- Don't offer a free audit in touch 1 — cheapens the frame
